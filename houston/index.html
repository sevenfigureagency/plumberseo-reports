Section 07 — Key Findings

What the Data Tells Us

FINDING 01
Reputation without visibility is invisible revenue
Seven of the 15 operators analyzed have 4.8–4.9 star ratings with hundreds of reviews. They are proven operators. Yet none have meaningful organic search visibility, and most hold only mid-tier Map Pack positions. In Houston's plumbing market, being the best isn't enough — you have to be findable.
FINDING 02
The market is less consolidated than it appears
Three companies hold 55% of traffic. But that means 45% is distributed across secondary and tertiary operators. The market is more fragmented than Dallas. There is room for new entrants to establish positions — if they move on secondary terms before consolidation accelerates.
FINDING 03
Conversion infrastructure is the hidden profit lever
68% of operators lack online booking. 40% lack live chat or SMS. These are not nice-to-haves — they are lead-killers. A prospect landing on your site at 11pm who can't book will move to whoever offers instant scheduling. Visibility brings the lead. Infrastructure converts it.
FINDING 04
Secondary terms are the path to market entry
Primary terms ("plumber Houston") are held by established operators. Secondary terms (drain cleaning, water heater repair, sewer repair, emergency plumber) have more open Map Pack positions and lower competition intensity. Operators who focus on secondary terms for 90 days can build momentum for a primary-term push in 2027.
