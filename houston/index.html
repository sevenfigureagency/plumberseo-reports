What the Analysis Shows

FINDING 01
Maps Dominance Is Not Balanced by Organic
Cooper Plumbing ranks #1 in Maps but doesn't appear in organic top 10. Texas Quality Plumbing is #3 in Maps but has zero organic visibility. This creates an opening — companies building organic strength can defend maps positions more effectively than companies relying on Maps alone.
FINDING 02
Reviews Are Concentrated, Not Distributed
Abacus Plumbing has 11,734 reviews. John Moore Services has 19,065 reviews. Village Plumbing has 9,523. Together, these three account for 40,322 reviews. The rest of the market shares 23,678. Companies with fewer than 100 reviews cannot compete on trust signal alone.
FINDING 03
Conversion Infrastructure Is Completely Missing
Every single company in the top 20 lacks online booking, live chat, or SMS response capability. This is not a competitive differentiator — it's an industry-wide gap. The first company to implement booking + chat stands out immediately in a market where 100% of competitors are phone-only.
FINDING 04
Reputation Doesn't Guarantee Visibility
Village Plumbing (9,523 reviews), John Moore Services (19,065 reviews), and Abacus Plumbing (11,734 reviews) should dominate search. Instead, they share the market with companies that have fewer than 500 reviews. Reputation matters, but visibility is determined by Maps optimization and organic strategy, not review count alone.
