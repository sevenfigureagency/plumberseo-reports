Why They're Winning

The companies at the top of this market didn't get there by accident. Here's what separates them from everyone else in the Indianapolis plumbing space.

Google Business Profile
Hope Plumbing and Benjamin Franklin Plumbing both maintain complete, verified Google Business Profiles with regular photo updates, detailed service categories, and responsive messaging. Their profiles show response times measured in hours, not days. Companies like Mr. Plumber (4,604 reviews) don't appear in the Maps Pack at all — a critical missed opportunity.
Review Velocity
The dominant players accumulate 15–25 new reviews per month. Hope Plumbing's 6,310 reviews and Benjamin Franklin's 5,865 reviews represent years of consistent customer acquisition and systematic review capture. Newer entrants like Carter's My Plumber (1,184 reviews) are growing faster per month but started from a lower base. Review count is a ranking signal that compounds over time.
Service Pages
Hope Plumbing and Benjamin Franklin maintain dedicated pages for water heater repair, drain cleaning, emergency service, and plumbing installation. This keyword-specific content pages ladder up to their authority for broad searches. Invisible competitors like Mr. Plumber show one undifferentiated homepage presence.
Backlink Authority
The dominant companies show up in local business directories, industry citations, and city/region-specific guides. This creates referring domain diversity that Google uses as a ranking factor. Companies with limited web presence lack this foundation and struggle to rank for competitive terms despite strong on-page optimization.

What the Analysis Shows

FINDING 01
Reputation Doesn't Equal Visibility
HomeSense has 1,552 five-star reviews. Chapman Heating has 6,968 reviews. Neither company appears in search results for any tracked keyword. Meanwhile, Hope Plumbing dominates with 6,310 reviews and strong Maps visibility. Review count alone doesn't drive visibility. Structure, consistency, and SEO strategy do.
FINDING 02
Maps Visibility Doesn't Build Organic Authority
Benjamin Franklin Plumbing holds Maps #2 for most keywords but doesn't rank on the first page of Google organic results. Hamm & Sons appears in both Maps and organic. Mr. Plumber ranks #1 organic for emergency plumbing but is invisible in Maps. These are not correlated — each requires separate strategy and execution.
FINDING 03
The Top 3 Control 65% of Available Calls
Hope Plumbing, Benjamin Franklin, and Carter's My Plumber combined capture an estimated 65% of all search traffic from the eight keywords tracked. The remaining 14 companies split 35%. A company at #4 in Maps gets significantly fewer calls than #3 — the dropoff is steep and compounds.
FINDING 04
Most Companies Have No Conversion Infrastructure
Zero of 17 companies offer online scheduling. Zero offer live chat or 2-way SMS. This means 100% of prospects must call during business hours to move forward. An estimated 30% of high-intent searches are lost to conversion friction — prospects leave and book with whoever makes it easiest.
