Executive Summary

How the Fort Worth Plumbing Market Currently Breaks Down

We analyzed 20 plumbing companies operating in the Fort Worth market. Our audit examined search visibility across Google Maps and organic results, review strength and velocity, website conversion features, and the concentration of digital demand across the competitive set.

Benjamin Franklin Plumbing, Water Heaters, & Drain Cleaning dominates the Maps Pack for high-intent terms with 2,354 verified reviews and consistent ranking in the #1 position. ClearWater Plumbers and Plumb Shield Plumbing hold positions #2 and #3 respectively, capturing the majority of local search traffic. A handful of other operators have secured secondary positions but lack organic presence.

Meanwhile, seven companies in this market have built exceptional reputation profiles—ratings of 4.9 to 5.0 stars and hundreds of verified reviews—yet appear nowhere when homeowners search for plumbing services. Their review quality suggests they do excellent work. Their search visibility suggests they are invisible to the digital marketplace.

This report breaks down where companies in the Fort Worth market currently stand, where the revenue concentration flows, and where operators can still move the needle with focused effort.

Market Positioning

How Plumbing Companies in Fort Worth Currently Break Down

After reviewing the market, most plumbing companies in Fort Worth fall into one of four categories.

Dominant
Strong search visibility and strong reputation. These companies appear consistently across high-value search terms and have the review volume to sustain their positions. They are capturing the largest share of digital leads in the market.
Plateaued
Strong reputation but limited search visibility. These companies have built excellent customer track records but are not appearing where homeowners are searching. Their reviews and ratings suggest quality work, but their digital presence does not reflect it.
Emerging
Growing search visibility with a developing reputation. These companies are investing in their digital presence and beginning to appear for key terms, but their review profiles are still maturing. They are on an upward trajectory.
Invisible
Low visibility and limited review presence. These companies may do good work but are not appearing in search results and lack the digital footprint to compete for new customers through online channels.

Section 07 — Observations

Three Patterns Across the Fort Worth Plumbing Market

PATTERN 01
Benjamin Franklin Plumbing dominates the Maps Pack with 2,354 reviews, but several competitors have strong reputation signals they are not leveraging. Seven companies in this analysis carry ratings of 4.9 to 5.0 stars and hundreds of verified reviews—yet they do not appear in search results. Their reputation is a hidden asset that could drive visibility if combined with basic search optimization.
PATTERN 02
The top three Maps Pack results capture the vast majority of visible search traffic, but the positions holding 65% of the market are not supported by comprehensive conversion infrastructure. Two-thirds of the operators analyzed lack online scheduling, and 70% have no chat or SMS capability. This friction creates opportunity for operators who invest in conversion tools.
PATTERN 03
Several high-reputation operators completely absent from Maps Pack and organic search (Team Enoch, ABC Plumbing, Drain Kings) represent invisible competition—they have customer proof points but zero digital discoverability. Rockwater Plumbing and Berkeys rank well organically but are missing from the Maps Pack entirely, leaving potential leads on the table.
Section 08 — Market Outlook

Why the Leaderboard May Still Shift

Benjamin Franklin Plumbing holds the #1 position with nearly 2,400 reviews—a significant advantage—yet many competing operators have matching or superior star ratings. Plumb Shield Plumbing and Mother Modern both maintain 5.0-star profiles, and Service Squad and Team Enoch carry 4.9+ ratings with 1,000+ reviews each. Review count is a factor in Maps ranking, but star quality is also considered. The dominance is real, but it is not unshakeable.

The Maps Pack positions held by companies in ranks #4 through #15 remain genuinely contestable. Several carry strong reputation signals (4.8–5.0 stars) but have not invested deeply in review growth or local citation management. A competitor that systematically improves Google Business Profile completeness, acquires 50–100 new reviews per quarter, and maintains consistent service-area citations can shift positions measurably within 90 days.

The most significant wildcard is the gap between organic and local search visibility. Berkeys ranks #1 organically but is missing from the Maps Pack entirely. Rockwater ranks #8 organically but has zero Maps presence. These operators are not competing for the segment of demand that drives the highest conversion rates—and neither are the companies fighting for #8 through #15 in the Maps Pack. The leaderboard is not locked in the way it appears.

Section 09 — Key Findings

What the Analysis Shows

FINDING 01
Strong reputation signals go entirely uncompensated in Fort Worth search
Seven companies in this analysis carry 4.9 to 5.0-star ratings with 100+ verified reviews each. None appear consistently in either the Maps Pack or organic results for high-intent plumbing terms. Their customers give them excellent marks. Google does not see them at all. This represents either a significant optimization failure or incomplete citation/profile data.
FINDING 02
Organic visibility is disconnected from Maps ranking
Berkeys ranks #1 organically but is completely absent from the Maps Pack. Rockwater ranks #8 organically but has zero Maps presence. This mismatch suggests different ranking factors are at play, and operators investing in only one channel are leaving leads on the table.
FINDING 03
Maps Pack conversion infrastructure is weak across the market
Only 2 of 20 companies analyzed offer online scheduling. Only 1 offers any real-time SMS response. The three companies with the most visible search presence do not offer either feature. Homeowners can find a plumber instantly but must call to book. Every friction point removes potential conversions.
FINDING 04
The market concentration is high, but positions #4–15 remain genuinely contestable
Benjamin Franklin, ClearWater, and Plumb Shield control 68% of visible digital demand. But companies in positions #6 through #15 carry strong reputation profiles and could shift positions with focused optimization effort. Review growth, citation consistency, and Google Business Profile completeness are the levers that move the needle.
