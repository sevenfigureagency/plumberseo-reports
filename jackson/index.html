What the Analysis Shows

FINDING 01
Reputation ≠ Visibility
Hiller Plumbing has 2,244 five-star reviews. Tim Ferguson has 1,681. Neither appears in Maps or organic rankings. Meanwhile, Environment Masters with 2,487 reviews holds #1 across most terms. Review count alone doesn't guarantee visibility — optimization and consistency do.
FINDING 02
Mid-Market Invisibility Is the Biggest Gap
Between "dominant" (Environment Masters, Roto-Rooter, DrainGo) and "invisible" (non-ranking companies), there's almost no middle ground. Mid-tier companies like Skeen, Jackson Plumbing & Drain Services, and Cornerstone have limited visibility. One strong push could move them into contention.
FINDING 03
Organic Ranking Isn't a Priority for Most Competitors
Only three companies rank on organic page 1. Most rely entirely on Maps, which means they're one algorithm update away from visibility collapse. Companies investing in both Maps AND organic search (like Roto-Rooter and DrainGo) have more resilience.
FINDING 04
Specialized Terms Have Lower Competition
Generic terms like "Jackson plumber" are crowded. But "drain cleaning Jackson" (90 searches/month) and "water heater repair Jackson" (70 searches/month) show lower competitive density. A company could own these verticals with targeted effort.
