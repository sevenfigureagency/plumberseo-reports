Section 07 — Key Findings

What the Data Tells Us

FINDING 01
Elite reputation without visibility is invisible revenue
Seven companies in Jacksonville have exceptional ratings — Premier Plumbing (5.0 stars, 416 reviews), Kingdom Based Plumbing (5.0 stars, 948 reviews), Betros (4.9 stars, 772 reviews). None of that reputation is working for them online. When a homeowner searches at 10pm, they do not find these operators. The most visible competitor wins, not the best operator.
FINDING 02
The conversion gap is as costly as the visibility gap
65% of Jacksonville plumbers lack online scheduling. 70% lack live chat or SMS. When a prospect lands and cannot book instantly, they move to the next result. Harry Hayes is the only high Map Pack operator with scheduling — and it shows in the data. Every friction point is a percentage of revenue walking to competitors.
FINDING 03
David Gray and Turner own the revenue because they own the Maps Pack
These two operators appear in the top 2 positions on nearly every primary term. For local intent searches, the Google Maps 3-pack captures 60–70% of all clicks. Maps dominance is maps revenue. That advantage compounds because high traffic drives more reviews, which drives more rankings, which drives more traffic.
FINDING 04
The window for secondary terms is genuinely open — but narrowing fast
Terms like "water heater repair Jacksonville" (140 searches/mo), "drain cleaning Jacksonville" (50 searches/mo), and neighborhood-specific searches remain genuinely contestable. A focused 90-day effort on GBP, review velocity, and service-area content can produce measurable Map Pack movement. That window will not remain open as market investment increases and consolidation accelerates.
