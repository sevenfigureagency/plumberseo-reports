Why They're Winning

The companies at the top of this market didn't get there by accident. Here's what separates them from everyone else.

Google Business Profile
The Waterworks maintains a complete, optimized Google Business Profile with consistent business hours, detailed service categories, high-quality photos, and recent posts. The Local Plumber and Mr. Rooter both show similar profile completeness. Comparison: Hesson Plumbing (4.9 stars, 822 reviews) has no visible profile or minimal activity, which explains why it doesn't appear in Maps despite strong reputation.
Review Velocity
Mr. Rooter Plumbing has accumulated 3,836 reviews (4.7 stars). The Waterworks has 2,159 reviews (4.7 stars). Both companies maintain steady review acquisition — a key Maps ranking factor. Review consistency signals active, credible operations. Companies like Gunther Plumbing and Accurate Plumbing have 792 and 118 reviews respectively but zero search visibility, showing reviews alone don't drive rankings without corresponding search signal strength.
Service Pages
Dominant companies appear in organic search for service-specific terms (drain cleaning, water heater repair, emergency plumbing). This suggests dedicated landing pages or content for each service. Parson Plumbing (5 stars, 537 reviews) ranks #1 organically for "plumber Columbus" because of service-focused content architecture. Most companies in the invisible category have no organic presence, indicating either no content strategy or poor on-page optimization.
Backlink Authority
Roto-Rooter (6,849 reviews) and Eco Plumbers (20,267 reviews) are national brands with significant backlink profiles from industry websites, directory listings, and partner sites. Local competitors cannot match this authority — which is why they compete on Maps and local content instead. Companies ranking in Maps Pack but not organic search have minimal backlink profiles; those ranking organically have some local citations and possibly partnership mentions.
