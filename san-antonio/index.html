Section 07 — Key Findings

What the Data Tells Us

FINDING 01
Reputation without visibility is revenue left on the table
Chambliss Plumbing (5.0 stars, 1,429 reviews), 1-Tom-Plumber (5.0 stars, 1,337 reviews), and five others have exceptional customer ratings. They rank #11–13 in Maps or are not visible at all online. A homeowner searching at midnight does not see those reviews—and the call goes to whoever appears at the top. The best plumber in San Antonio is not necessarily winning. The most visible one is.
FINDING 02
The market is not locked—it is defended
George Plumbing Co., 1st Call Plumbing & AC, and Mr. Plumber hold dominant positions because they invested early in Maps. Those positions are not unbreakable—they are maintained. A sustained effort against secondary terms (drain cleaning, water heater repair, emergency plumber) can produce measurable shifts within 90 days. The window is still open for operators willing to invest.
FINDING 03
Conversion infrastructure is nearly absent from this market
Of 20 companies analyzed, only one—Mr. Plumber—offers online scheduling. None offer live chat or two-way SMS. That means nearly every visitor to a competitor's site must pick up the phone or wait for a callback to book. In a market where immediacy wins the job, this is a catastrophic gap. The operator who fixes this first gains a structural conversion advantage.
FINDING 04
Four high-value secondary terms are genuinely open
Drain cleaning, water heater repair, emergency plumber, and sewer repair generate 1,660 combined monthly searches. The top 3 spots on these terms are not held by the same companies holding primary terms. The positioning is more distributed, which means a focused 90-day effort on review velocity, GBP completeness, and service-area content can produce measurable ranking shifts. This is the realistic window.
